where to buy · what to ask · how to choose

every bolt tells a story. here's how to read the label, feel the weight, and find the source.

flax / linen

Linen Hall · Belarusian flax, mid-weight
thread count: 80–120

hand & structure

  • stones-washed linen gets softer – ask if pre-treated.
  • avoid "linen touch" – it's usually polyester.
  • fullness: holds shape, drapes without being clingy.
  • local: “Lyon” market Komaroŭski, linen row 3.
⚡ boil if you want extra shrinkage, then line dry.

wool / fleece

local mill · 100% virgin wool

assess before buy

  • pilling resistance: tightly twisted fibres last longer.
  • layering weight: 180–220 gsm for indoors.
  • superwash? loses some warmth but machine safe.
  • stall “Ceply kutočak” upper gallery, Kamaroŭski.
storage: keep in cotton bag with lavender.

cotton · woven & jersey

organic cotton / deadstock prints

dress / shirt fabric

  • end-on-end: subtle texture for shirting.
  • voile: lightweight, semi-sheer – good for summer.
  • shrinkage: sanforized labelled = less than 3%.
  • second‑hand gem: “Haspadar” Čyrvanona 12, big cotton table.

denim · indigo

Japanese selvedge · 14.5 oz

what to look for

  • slubby uneven yarns = character, not flaw.
  • indigo bleed is normal – first wash separate.
  • cinch back? vintage style, allows adjustment.
  • “Ruptura” vul. Kisialiova 19, basement level.
soak in vinegar water to set indigo first time.

leather · bark tan

Italian veg‑tan · 2.5–3 mm

leather quality

  • temper: should be stiff but not cardboard.
  • flesh side: smooth suede-like or rough? both ok.
  • cutting: ask for bend or shoulder – firmer.
  • “Škura” pavilion, market Ushodni, stall 41.

silk · mulberry / wild

mulberry charmeuse 19 mm

real vs fake

  • ring test: pull silk through ring – real silk glides smoothly.
  • water test: drop water – real absorbs fast, synthetic beads.
  • lining: real silk is anti-static, cool in summer.
  • “Clothes for Friends” now has silk remnants box.

mending · cloth remnants

haberdashery “Zolka” new arrivals

keep these in your kit

  • speedweve or darning loom for jeans.
  • waxed thread (linen) for leather repairs.
  • fusible interfacing scraps – stabilise thin spots.
  • “Niki” vul. Kirava 25 — cheap mending corner.
mend before you toss — three stitches save a garment.

staple · quick reference

🧶 hand: rough = durable outerwear; smooth = next‑to‑skin.
🧺 shrink: natural fibres always shrink — buy slightly loose.
⚖️ weight: heavy = warm / structured; light = drapes / layers.
📍 source: local first — you can return, repair, trust.
